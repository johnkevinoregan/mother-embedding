## 1. Keypoint & shape diagnosticity (`KeyPointDiagnosticity.md` / `.jl`)

360 EMNIST instances, 12 classes; η² per feature + leave-one-out nearest-class-mean.

- **Global shape harmonics carry the identity** — ~57 % alone (≈7× chance), ~61 %
  extended to `|M1..6|` + a radial (filled-vs-hollow) profile. `|M2|,|M3|,|M4|` are
  the strongest single features (η² ≈ 0.6).
- **Local keypoint *counts* are weak** (16–24 %): a census of types discards *where*
  each keypoint is, and configuration is what separates the letters.
- Best detector was the **branch profile** `B_φ(p) = min(E_θ(φ)(p), E_θ(φ)(p+d·u(φ)))`;
  branches = angular maxima, type = count + angles. A **medial/spine gate** (du Buf
  2006; Heitger 1992; Würtz & Lourens 2000) halved the outline-tracing phantom
  corners (combined LOO 49.7 → 51.9 %). Literature scan added (incl. Xia–Delon–Gousseau
  **ACJ** a-contrario junctions; "what to borrow from ACJ").
- Three structural lessons that held through everything: **(1) rectify first** — no
  linear filter combination detects termination; **(2) conjunctions must be gated
  (`min`), not summed; (3)** each question needs its own gate and scale.

## 2. Even/odd channel split — a clean negative result (`EvenOddChannels.md`)

Motivation: a hard requirement that *every detector give identical results on
polarity-inverted EMNIST with no algorithm change.* Split oriented energy into
**even/line** `L = |Ce|` and **odd/edge** `Ed = |Co|` (each pools its own ON/OFF pair,
so each is exactly polarity-invariant by the DC-free argument; measured interior
invariance 1.2e-6).

- **H2 (endpoints):** `min(L_θ, Ed_{θ+90})` should fire at ends, reject crossings.
  **Failed, structurally, not by tuning:** an **end-cap and a crossing stroke's flank
  are the same oriented edge** — same orientation, same phase. Only *termination*
  (what lies beyond the edge) separates them, which is what the branch/energy
  detector already measures. So the phase channel adds nothing for endpoints.
- Also measured **EMNIST stroke width ≈ 13.4 px** (upsampled 112²): letters are only
  ~8 stroke-widths across, so there is almost no scale separation — a structural
  constraint on the whole local-keypoint programme.
- **H1** (the `L` channel for the junction/branch side) remains **untested**.

## 3. Interactive endpoint detectors (`EndpointDiagnostics*.jl`)

Two Pluto notebooks with sliders + heatmaps to *understand* the even/odd endpoint
detector rather than just score it. `EndpointDiagnosticsPadded.jl` is the current one.

- **Detector:** `S(q) = max_{φ,p} min(|Ce_θ(φ)(p)|, cap(φ))` deposited at
  `q = p + δ·u(φ)`, with a **sign-referenced cap** `cap = [−sign(Ce)·κ·ds·Co_⊥]₊`
  (polarity-specific "falling edge ahead" yet globally invariant), δ tied to the line
  filter's half-length (`δ = dratio·σ_T`), and the score attributed to the **end-cap
  location** `p+δu`, not the source.
- **Padded 224×224 field** (letter 112 + border 56): makes edge-replicate padding
  *exact* (stroke ends sit in genuine background) and displays kernels at true scale.
- Confirmed the sign-referenced detector is **exactly polarity-invariant** (2e-6).
  The chronic weakness remains **over-firing on flanks** (the `max_φ` picks
  across-stroke directions that land the probe on a stroke's own flank).

## 4. Comparison with Ronan's classical end-stopping (`CommentsOnRonanLiquid.md`)

Ronan's notebook (`RonanLiquid1.jl`) does end-stopping the **textbook hypercomplex
way**: a recurrent Dale's-law **E/I sheet** (on-centre/off-surround, emergent
Mexican-hat) → rectified oriented **line** simple cells → **symmetric subtractive**
end-inhibition `[S_θ − β(S_θ(+Δ)+S_θ(−Δ))]₊`, read at the dominant orientation.
Added as an **A/B toggle** in our notebook. Findings, all measured:

- His Stage-3 ↔ our energy "Detector B" — same "centre − displaced, rectified"
  skeleton — but symmetric vs directional, line-only vs even/odd, ON-only vs
  polarity-invariant.
- **β is critical.** On our broad Gabor energy, Ronan's own default **β=0.7 kills the
  true ends** (the behind-flank is full-strength while the end-response is on its
  down-ramp); **β≈0.5** is the working regime. His sharply-peaked `hw≈5` cell
  tolerates 0.7; our `σ_T=12` kernel doesn't.
- **Front-end normalisation** (a sign-preserving `DoG` / `DoG+divisive` toggle):
  **thins the effective stroke 11.8 → 2.6 px** but **does not** widen the β-range —
  the end-stop reads the *Gabor's energy envelope*, whose peak width is the **filter
  footprint**, not the input stroke width. The lever is `w_L`; normalisation is
  orthogonal (§8, corrected — this was a falsified prediction).
- His boundary handling is plain **zero-padding** (per-tap bounds check), safe for him
  only because EMNIST's background is *literally 0* and his rectifying transfer keeps
  it 0; the Stage-1 `gauss_weights` renormalisation is the one genuinely
  background-robust piece (§4).

## 5. A chirality bug in our detector — found and fixed (§5 of the Comments doc)

The most important concrete result of the period.

- **Symptom:** on a perfectly symmetric bar the endpoint keypoints came out
  asymmetric. Measured: the map is **exactly 180°-rotation symmetric** (1e-6) but
  **~41 % mirror-asymmetric**. The local-max extractor was innocent (no ties); the
  chirality is in the `cap`.
- **Root cause:** the cap's sign factor was `ds = (φ < π ? +1 : −1)`, a **half-plane in
  the travel angle**. But `perp(t)` folds the perpendicular orientation into `[0,π)`,
  so the odd filter's carrier flips relative to the stroke at the **90° fold** — which
  `ds` can't see. Going around the circle: correct in the 0–90° / 180–270° quadrants,
  **inverted** in 90–180° / 270–360°.
- **Why it hid:** 180°-symmetry is preserved (a stroke's two ends share the same
  quadrant pair, so they always agree — genuine endpoints fire correctly at *every*
  orientation), and the **max-over-24-directions** turns a wrong-sign direction into a
  weaker *fallback* to a correct neighbour rather than a hole — so on an O it shows as
  a smooth two-bright/two-dim ring, never a discontinuity. (A deliberately
  opposite-phase detector is ~3× weaker and uncorrelated, so the phase genuinely
  matters; the redundancy just masks it.)
- **Fix:** read the sign off the odd filter's own carrier,
  **`ds = sign(u(φ)·n̂_ψ) = sign(sin(φ − θ_ψ))`** (implemented as
  `sin(φ - THETAS[ψ]) >= 0 ? -1f0 : 1f0`; κ=+1 still means "falling"). **Verified:**
  mirror-asym **0.415 → 1.1e-6**, endpoint tips unchanged (21.475, both ends equal),
  O-ring **0.43 → 0.05**. It **restores symmetry but does not reduce over-firing** —
  bar keypoints go **11 → 13** (the previously-suppressed diagonal of corners now fires
  too). Consequence: the min-gate counts in `EvenOddChannels.md` §8 are now **stale**.

## 6. Gaussian curvature of the intensity surface (`GaussianCurvatureEMNIST.jl`) — new

Treats the image as a height surface `z = I(x,y)` and shows
`K = (I_xx I_yy − I_xy²)/(1 + I_x² + I_y²)²` at five log-spaced scales, on the same
224 field, from Gaussian-derivative jets.

- **Polarity-invariant by construction** (every derivative flips sign; K is products
  and squares of them) — verified 5.8e-10, no calibration constant, and **no
  chirality** (built from the isotropic Hessian).
- **Measured finding (and a corrected note):** the **sign does *not* separate ends
  from crossings.** At σ≈6 *everything* salient is `K>0` (elliptic) — end ≈ +46,
  junction/crossing ≈ +15, corner ≈ +24 — because a smoothed crossing is the
  *brightest* point (arms pile up), a local max, not a saddle. Straight strokes ≈ 0.
  So K is a **blob/keypoint saliency** (≈ det-Hessian); what separates feature types
  is **magnitude and the scale of the peak**, not the sign.

## Structural state / what stands

- **Polarity invariance** is achievable and now routine (DC-free kernels + energy/abs,
  or products-of-derivatives). Both the sign-referenced cap and Gaussian curvature
  satisfy the requirement exactly.
- **Endpoint detection via oriented energy is real but limited** (cap = flank; only
  termination separates end from crossing), and the operators are **too broad relative
  to the stroke** (§2 measurement) — the scale constraint, not tuning, bounds the whole
  local programme.
- The **hard-typing frame** (corner-vs-curve is a continuum) is still the suspected
  residual, unaddressed by any of the above.

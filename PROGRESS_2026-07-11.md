# mother-embedding — progress summary, 2026-07-11

Continues from `PROGRESS_2026-07-09.md`.

## Corrected (2026-07-12): kernels are now zero-DC

`gabor_kernel` now subtracts the kernel mean before normalizing, so the
complex kernel has **exactly zero DC**. This corrects a slight error: the
even/cosine channel previously carried a small residual DC (~1.5% of the
ideal-response scale at σ=0.5λ — the "~1% DC leak" flagged under Session
findings below). A uniform region of value `c` therefore leaked a spurious
response ∝ `c`, inflating its modulus and biasing its phase toward "line".

It was harmless in practice only because EMNIST backgrounds are black
(`c ≈ 0`, so the leak is ≈ 0), which is why it went unnoticed — but it was
still wrong, and any bright uniform region would have produced a phantom
even-channel response. Mean-subtraction makes every uniform region, at any
brightness, give exactly 0.

The positive-lobe (ideal-response) normalizer is re-derived *after* the
subtraction, since deepening the negative lobes changes the positive-lobe
sum. Verified: post-fix `|DC| ≈ 1e-7` and the positive lobe still sums to
1.0 at every scale. Downstream impact on black-background EMNIST is
negligible — per-scale max moduli on the test "T" are unchanged at
0.15 / 0.35 / 0.63 / 0.47 (λ=3/6/12/24). The change is baked into the
kernel, so no call site needed touching.

## New (2026-07-12): configurable Gabor anisotropy (`GABOR_ASPECT`)

`gabor_kernel` grew an `aspect` keyword (defaulting to the new Config
constant `GABOR_ASPECT`) controlling the envelope's elongation: it is the
ratio of the σ **along** the contour to the σ **across** it.

- `aspect = 1` → the original isotropic (circular) envelope.
- `aspect > 1` → elongated along the stroke (narrower orientation tuning,
  simple-cell-like), while the across-contour σ — hence the carrier
  bandwidth / scale selectivity — is left untouched.

This replaces the old unused `γ` keyword, whose sense was inverted (γ
compressed the tangent axis) and which never enlarged the kernel support,
so an elongated envelope would have been truncated. `aspect` grows the
support with `ceil(2σ·max(1, aspect))`, so the longer envelope is fully
represented; at `aspect = 1` the support and every kernel value are
**identical to before** (verified: EMNIST "T" per-scale max moduli
0.146 / 0.353 / 0.626 / 0.469 at aspect 1, matching the recorded values;
kernel support 25²→73² and still zero-DC / lobe-normalized at aspect 3).
`aspect` threads through `gabor_lift` (default `GABOR_ASPECT`); the
T-junction path needs no change since `t_junction_lift` just consumes the
Gabor samples.

To explore it, all three test notebooks (`Test_CreateGaborLifting`, both
`Test_CreateTJunctionLifting*`) got a **Gabor-aspect slider** (1.0–4.0,
default 1.0) wired into their `gabor_lift(...; aspect=…)` call. The
overlay glyphs were made to reflect the elongation honestly:

- The filled stroke ellipses scale their along-contour semi-axis by
  `aspect` (across-contour unchanged), so a bigger aspect visibly stretches
  each glyph along the bar it is tuned to.
- In the T-junction notebooks the dashed receptive-field outline is now an
  **ellipse** (λ across, λ·aspect along the stem direction) rather than a
  radius-λ circle — reducing to the original circle at aspect 1.

At `aspect = 1` every figure is visually and numerically identical to
before this parameter existed.

## Changed: kernels are now ideal-response normalized (not energy normalized)

`gabor_kernel` no longer applies the usual energy normalization (Σ|K|² = 1);
instead the kernel itself is scaled so that its response to its **ideal
achievable stimulus** — a white (value 1) bar exactly filling the positive
lobe, on black, the best any [0,1]-valued image can present — equals 1.
(Went through an intermediate step where `gabor_lift` divided each modulus
by an exported `ideal_response(kernel)`; since correlation is linear, that
divisor was then folded into the kernel itself, removing the extra constant
from every call site.) A modulus is therefore directly the *fraction of the
best achievable response*: a perfectly matched full-contrast bar reads
**1.0 at every scale** (verified end-to-end through `gabor_lift` at all four
scales), and blank background reads 0. `gabor_lift` applies no correction
at all anymore — the λ division is gone. A code comment at the
normalization line records that skipping energy normalization is
deliberate.

Why this replaces /λ: dividing by λ already flattened the ideal-stimulus
response across scales, but only because that response happens to grow
roughly ∝ λ — the ideal bar landed at ≈ 0.51–0.59 rather than at a
meaningful value. The new divisor is the same normalization with the
arbitrary constant removed, so values are directly interpretable.
Rankings and cross-scale behavior are essentially unchanged (the two
divisors differ by a nearly scale-independent factor ≈ 2): on the EMNIST
test "T" the strongest T-junction candidate is still the true junction
(x=23, y=23, α=270°, scale 3), per-scale max moduli are 0.14 / 0.35 /
0.63 / 0.47 for λ=3/6/12/24.

`View_GaborKernels.jl` shows the same convention in its bar panel and
measured-response table ("fraction of ideal") — with the normalization
baked into the kernels, the raw correlation values are already in these
units.

## Session findings (Q&A driving the change, verified numerically)

- Input images have pixels in [0, 1] (EMNIST bytes ÷ 255); artificial test
  images must use the same range. The synthetic T in the viewer notebook
  briefly had strokes at 2 (out of range, doubling all responses) — reset
  to 1.
- Energy normalization (Σ|K|² = 1) does **not** cap responses at 1: the
  Cauchy–Schwarz bound |r| ≤ 1 applies only to unit-L2-energy image
  patches. A full-white stroke has patch energy ≈ 10, so raw responses of
  ~4.5 (λ=12) are correct arithmetic, not a bug.
- Contrast polarity: inverting the image (black T on white) negates the
  complex response — same modulus (up to a ~1% DC leak), phase shifted
  180°. The T-junction detector is therefore polarity-invariant (both
  phases flip together, so Δphase and strength are unchanged), while a
  junction whose two strokes have *opposite* polarity is suppressed by the
  compatibility factor. Normalization choice cannot affect any of this
  (it is a per-kernel constant, applied after the image is seen).

## Viewer notebook display fix

The synthetic-T overlay previously drew the image at half brightness and
*added* the kernel tint, which is why full-range strokes looked gray (and
why saturating them to 2 seemed necessary). The overlay now renders the
image at true brightness and **blends** each pixel toward pure red
(positive kernel weight) or pure blue (negative), so the tint is visible
on white strokes and black background alike.

## Gabor test-notebook display fix

`Test_CreateGaborLifting.jl` drew every sample line with a minimum opacity
of 0.15, so zero-modulus samples in black regions appeared as faint cyan
lines (zero response → phase 0 → hue 180° = cyan), suggesting non-zero
responses where there are none (verified: a kernel window that is entirely
black gives modulus exactly 0). Both figures now make opacity directly
proportional to the modulus — a zero response is invisible. Remaining faint
lines just off the strokes are genuine small responses to the antialiased
stroke fringe (dim gray pixels that read as black next to the white
strokes).

## Gabor test-notebook fixes and display overhaul

- **Opacity floor removed**: both figures drew every sample line at a
  minimum opacity of 0.15, so zero-modulus samples in black regions showed
  as faint cyan lines (zero response → phase 0 → hue 180°). Opacity is now
  strictly proportional to the modulus; a zero response draws nothing.
  (Verified: a kernel window that is entirely black gives modulus exactly
  0; the faint near-stroke samples that remain are genuine responses to
  the antialiased stroke fringe.)
- **Orientation bug fixed**: overlay segments used (sin θ, cos θ) — the
  carrier axis — so θ=0 (the horizontal-bar detector) drew vertical lines,
  and oblique θ were mirrored. Now (cos θ, sin θ): the segment shows the
  bar the filter responds to, matching the T-junction glyph convention.
- **Glyphs are now ellipses**, 2λ/3 × λ/3 (2/3 of the tuned-stroke
  footprint λ × λ/2, shrunk so neighbors at the 0.75λ grid spacing stay
  distinguishable), oriented along θ.
- **Filter-bank grid**: phase → hue (same cyclic map as the single-filter
  figure); opacity normalized to the *global* max modulus over the whole
  bank (moduli share the fraction-of-ideal scale, so panels are
  comparable — a pale panel genuinely responds less); layout reshaped to
  4 panels per row (Pluto caps the page at ~700 px, so narrow-and-tall
  wins; a `main`-width CSS override was tried and abandoned — it clips
  off-window content unrecoverable).

## T-junction notebooks: same glyph language

Both T-junction figures now use the same conventions: stem and crossbar
drawn as 2λ/3 × λ/3 ellipses (stem centered on the sampled point pointing
along α, crossbar centered on the neighbor sample point), hue = stem
phase, opacity ∝ strength normalized globally (per figure) — replacing
the saturation-stretch coding. Two bugs caught by KO'R along the way:
diagonal stems were √2 larger (sized by the neighbor-distance vector);
and `maximum(...; init=1f0)` clamped the global max to 1, rendering all
glyphs uniformly faint (init must be 0). Verified the panel normalization
is honest on the EMNIST "A": per-panel max alphas 0.40 / 1.00 / 0.94 /
0.48 — the impression that scale 4 is "as opaque as the rest" comes from
overlap compositing (9 huge glyphs tiling the panel), not the alphas.

## New: `Test_CreateTJunctionLifting_SyntheticT.jl`

Cell-for-cell copy of the T-junction test notebook with EMNIST replaced
by the synthetic T from `View_GaborKernels.jl` — a controlled stimulus
for studying the combined stem/crossbar responses. Findings so far:

- The strongest candidates are **not** at the junction: scale-1
  detections at stroke *ends* (bottom of the stem, crossbar tips) win at
  ~0.31, vs ~0.22 for the true junction (scale 4). At λ=3 (smaller than
  the 4 px stroke width) both filters respond to the edges of a single
  stroke with compatible phases — the corner/end-firing property beats
  the real junction.
- Scale 3 (λ=12, grid columns …23, 32…) has no sample on the stem
  (column 28.5): grid alignment can make a scale miss a feature entirely.
- With the crossbar zeroed (stem only), scale-4 candidates appear whose
  glyphs lie wholly in black, 17.5 px from the stem — genuine responses:
  the λ=24 kernel window is 49 px wide, so the stem is inside their
  receptive field (vertical-filter modulus 0.13 measured there), and both
  off-stem filters see the *same* stem, so their phases are coherent and
  the compatibility factor is high. The detector measures "compatible
  responses at two grid points", not "two strokes physically present".

To make that visible, **every candidate now gets a dashed circle of
radius λ** (the kernel's Gaussian extent, 2σ) around its stem point, in
both T-junction notebooks — at scale 4 the circles of the "black area"
candidates visibly sweep across the stem.

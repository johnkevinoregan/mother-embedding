# mother-embedding — progress summary, 2026-07-11

Continues from `PROGRESS_2026-07-09.md`.

## Changed: kernels are now ideal-response normalized (not energy normalized)

`gabor_kernel` no longer applies the usual energy normalization (Σ|K|² = 1);
instead the kernel itself is scaled so that its response to its **ideal
achievable stimulus** — a white (value 1) bar exactly filling the positive
lobe, on black, the best any [0,1]-valued image can present — equals 1.
(Went through an intermediate step where `gabor_lift` divided each modulus
by an exported `ideal_response(kernel)`; since correlation is linear, that
divisor was then folded into the kernel itself, removing the extra constant
from every call site.) A modulus is therefore directly the *fraction of the
best achievable response*: a perfectly matched full-contrast bar reads
**1.0 at every scale** (verified end-to-end through `gabor_lift` at all four
scales), and blank background reads 0. `gabor_lift` applies no correction
at all anymore — the λ division is gone. A code comment at the
normalization line records that skipping energy normalization is
deliberate.

Why this replaces /λ: dividing by λ already flattened the ideal-stimulus
response across scales, but only because that response happens to grow
roughly ∝ λ — the ideal bar landed at ≈ 0.51–0.59 rather than at a
meaningful value. The new divisor is the same normalization with the
arbitrary constant removed, so values are directly interpretable.
Rankings and cross-scale behavior are essentially unchanged (the two
divisors differ by a nearly scale-independent factor ≈ 2): on the EMNIST
test "T" the strongest T-junction candidate is still the true junction
(x=23, y=23, α=270°, scale 3), per-scale max moduli are 0.14 / 0.35 /
0.63 / 0.47 for λ=3/6/12/24.

`View_GaborKernels.jl` shows the same convention in its bar panel and
measured-response table ("fraction of ideal") — with the normalization
baked into the kernels, the raw correlation values are already in these
units.

## Session findings (Q&A driving the change, verified numerically)

- Input images have pixels in [0, 1] (EMNIST bytes ÷ 255); artificial test
  images must use the same range. The synthetic T in the viewer notebook
  briefly had strokes at 2 (out of range, doubling all responses) — reset
  to 1.
- Energy normalization (Σ|K|² = 1) does **not** cap responses at 1: the
  Cauchy–Schwarz bound |r| ≤ 1 applies only to unit-L2-energy image
  patches. A full-white stroke has patch energy ≈ 10, so raw responses of
  ~4.5 (λ=12) are correct arithmetic, not a bug.
- Contrast polarity: inverting the image (black T on white) negates the
  complex response — same modulus (up to a ~1% DC leak), phase shifted
  180°. The T-junction detector is therefore polarity-invariant (both
  phases flip together, so Δphase and strength are unchanged), while a
  junction whose two strokes have *opposite* polarity is suppressed by the
  compatibility factor. Normalization choice cannot affect any of this
  (it is a per-kernel constant, applied after the image is seen).

## Viewer notebook display fix

The synthetic-T overlay previously drew the image at half brightness and
*added* the kernel tint, which is why full-range strokes looked gray (and
why saturating them to 2 seemed necessary). The overlay now renders the
image at true brightness and **blends** each pixel toward pure red
(positive kernel weight) or pure blue (negative), so the tint is visible
on white strokes and black background alike.

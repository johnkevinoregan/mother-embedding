## Resolved: cross-scale strength comparability (issue 2 from yesterday)

`gabor_lift` now divides each modulus by the kernel wavelength λ
(`CreateGaborLifting.jl`). Energy-normalized kernels alone still integrate
more image energy at larger scales — measured max modulus 1.20 / 3.85 / 5.82
at λ=6/12/24 on a real EMNIST "T", roughly proportional to λ. After the
division the per-scale maxima are 0.20 / 0.32 / 0.24, essentially flat.

Effect on T-junctions: the combined top-30 ranking previously contained zero
smallest-scale candidates (scale composition 0/16/14); afterwards it spans
all scales, and the single strongest candidate on the test "T" is the true
junction — a stem point on the vertical stroke pointing up (α=270°) at its
grid neighbor sitting right at the crossbar. (The stem glyph is anchored one
grid step below the junction; that is the detector's geometry, not a
position error.)

## New: `View_GaborKernels.jl` — kernel/pair viewer notebook

A notebook for understanding what the detectors actually compute, built in
response to "what underlying pattern does a strong T response indicate, and
what role does phase play?". Three interactive figures (red = positive
weights, blue = negative, throughout):

1. **Single Gabor kernel** at a chosen scale/orientation/phase. The pattern
   displayed for phase φ is `real(kernel·e^(−iφ))` — exactly the image
   pattern that would produce a response with phase φ, so the phase slider
   sweeps through the family of templates the complex kernel scores at once.
2. **T-junction kernel pair** — stem kernel at center, crossbar kernel one
   grid step away along α, the exact geometry `t_junction_lift` uses, each
   with its own phase slider.
3. **The pair slid over a synthetic T** (4 px strokes, junction at
   ≈ x=28, y=14), with x/y sliders for the stem kernel's position. Shown as
   **two separate overlays** — one per kernel — because the kernels are
   correlated with the image independently; an earlier version drew both
   into one summed composite, which was misleading (the summed colors in
   the overlap correspond to no computed quantity). A third panel shows 7
   bars on a fixed −1…+1 axis, all /λ-normalized: per kernel the signed
   projection onto the displayed template `P(φ)`, onto the quadrature
   template `P(φ+90°)`, and the modulus `|r| = √(P(φ)² + P(φ+90°)²)`; plus
   the combined feature `min(|r_s|,|r_c|) × (1+cos Δphase)/2`.

Key relationships the figure makes visible (verified numerically): the
projections respond to the phase sliders and go negative when the template
is anti-aligned; `P(φ)` reaches the modulus exactly when φ equals the
*measured* phase (`atan2(P(90°), P(0°))` — the direction of the response
vector, undefined where the modulus is ~0, e.g. on blank background); the
modulus and the combined feature are phase-slider-invariant, since the
modulus is the best projection over all phases. This underpins the design
discussion of what to transmit upward: modulus is the trustworthy "something
is here" signal; phase is meaningful only conditional on modulus and is best
consumed in local comparisons (as the T-junction compatibility factor does)
rather than transmitted raw.

## Observation (no action taken)

In the T-junction figures the four diagonal α directions are systematically
weaker than the cardinals (the diagonal neighbor is √2× farther, so its
crossbar sample is farther from the stem): top-30 on the test "T" splits 21
cardinal / 9 diagonal, and the diagonals render pale. This can make the
figures read as "only 4 directions". Note the Gabor orientation count does
not appear in these figures at all — glyph direction is α, the 8 neighbor
compass directions.
